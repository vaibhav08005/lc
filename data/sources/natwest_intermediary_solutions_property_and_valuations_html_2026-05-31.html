Property and Valuations

We can look to lend on a range of different property types including ex-local authority homes, properties above or near commercial premises and freehold flats.

At a glance

The property must be:

  • Readily saleable & mortgageable for owner occupation
  • In England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland and the Isles of Scilly.

Where a property has been built, converted or significantly renovated within the last 10 years, we will require a warranty to be in place. Please see our New Build Hub for acceptable warranties and further guidance.

All properties are subject to our valuers assessment of condition, marketability and suitability to our lending criteria. 

Minimum property value

We will base the minimum property value on the purchase price or the valuation figure, whichever is lower.

Property criteria: A-E

Acreage

We don’t have a maximum plot size.


Where a property is over 4 hectares/10 acres, we will be able to consider the property subject to underwriter assessment and a suitable valuation report.

Annex

Properties with an annex may be considered subject to valuer’s comments, where it is intended that a family member will reside in the annex or where this will be used as family staff accommodation e.g. live-in childcare arrangements. In addition to standard valuation requirements, the valuer will consider the following when assessing the property:

  • Potential for letting: We can't lend on a property where the annex could be sublet on a permanent or temporary basis. 

  • Access and service: An annex should normally share its access and services with the main residence. If these are separate the risk of the annex being sublet is increased and may result in the property being declined. 

  • Size and nature: The annex should be secondary to the main residence. For example a 6 bedroom house with a 1 bedroom annex could be considered however a 3 bedroom house with a 2 bedroom cottage is likely to be unacceptable.  

  • Buy to let: Where the property will be a buy to let, this can only be considered where there is likely to be sufficient demand from potential tenants who wish to occupy the whole property as a single- family residence.

Coach house (freehold)

We treat coach houses the same as ‘Freehold Flats/Flying Freehold Properties’, please see this section for full details.

Deck/Balcony access

Can be considered subject to valuer’s comments. 

Please see Ex-local authority flats (below) where relevant. 

Ex-local authority flats

Can be considered subject to valuer’s comments. The valuer will consider the demand for owner occupation.

Property criteria: F-L

Flats over commercial premises/Property near commercial

Can be considered subject to valuer’s comments. The valuer will consider aspects that may impact marketability such as: noise, smells, anti-social opening hours and fire risk in conjunction with locality and demand.

Floor area

Property must have a minimum floor area of 30m2. This will be determined by the valuer. The floor area excludes any balconies or covered external areas.

Freehold Flats/Flying Freehold Properties

Acceptable but only where it is possible to enforce positive covenants against other occupants of the building i.e. a written agreement is in place to ensure maintenance, repair and insurance of the building and common parts are shared equitably amongst all residents.

  • Maximum Loan to Value is restricted to 90% (75% for Buy to Lets)

  • For this type of property a standard valuation will be instructed

  • On a remortgage our free legal products may not be suitable for this type of property and where our appointed solicitors are able to act, additional costs could arise due to the extra work required. Natwest provides cashback option on our remortgage products to assist where customers wish or are required to use their own solicitors. All remortgages with this property type will continue to benefit from a free valuation. 

 

Property types we’ll consider are:

  • Freehold flat (Max 90% LTV). This includes coach houses
  • Flying freehold flat (Max 90% LTV)
  • Share of freehold flat
  • Leasehold flat where the customer owns the freehold (We will require security over the leasehold flat and the freehold interest.) 
  • Leasehold flat - customer owns freehold reversion (Tyneside) - Two leasehold flats in one building. Leasehold A owns the Freehold of Leasehold B and Leasehold B owns the Freehold of Leasehold A.

The valuation must confirm the property is readily saleable and a Solicitor confirms the Title is good and marketable.

Japanese Knotweed

  • The valuer will assess each case on an individual basis.  

For more information, please see our Japanese knotweed guide.

Property criteria: M-R

Mobile or Park Homes

Unacceptable

Multistorey flats/High-rises

Can be considered subject to valuer’s comments on their own merits.

Northern Ireland only - NIHE / ex-local authority flats

See Northern Ireland Hub.

One Bedroom properties in Northern Ireland

Unacceptable, unless the property is part of a purpose built apartment block located within the post code range BT1 to BT18. If they are within these postcodes a management company should be in existence.

See Northern Ireland Hub.

Residential Business use

If your customer intends to run a business from their property, we’ll provide a mortgage where:

  • The property will predominantly be used for residential use and can be readily restored in its entirety to owner occupation.

Predominantly is defined as  more than 50% residential use. We will rely on the valuer to confirm the predominant use of the property.

 

Typical examples where this is acceptable are:

  • Architects, Accountants, Chiropractors, Physiotherapists, running a business from an office or room, lodgers, artists, musicians, cook with a studio, or similar, linked to their occupation.
  • Temporary Holiday Lets - subject to maximum of 90 days in a rolling 12-month period. The customer must ensure they are not breaching any requirements or regulations of local or other relevant authorities.

Annexes are acceptable only if they are being used for reasons within the above acceptability examples.

 

Examples of business/commercial use that are not acceptable:

  • Bed and Breakfasts
  • Country Estates, Fishing or Hunting rights, Guesthouses, Kennels,  Equestrian centres, Property development purposes, Campsites, Croft, Forestry
  • Permanent Holiday let accommodation
  • Land being farmed or livestock kept for commercial purposes; regardless of whether this is to meet agricultural restrictions
  • Nursing Homes
  • Student accommodation

This is not an exhaustive.

 

Purpose built Live/work properties

Purpose built or converted properties with a registered Live/Work title are not acceptable, as the title restrictions on this type of property can affect future sale

Property criteria: S

Self-Build or built within the last 2 years as a Self-Build

The bank considers a property to be a Self-Build where; 

  • It meets our New Build definition, decided by the valuer at point of valuation and; 
  • Where there is no purchase transaction involved 
     

In England, Wales and Scotland, we can’t consider any requests to repay self-build lending a customer has taken with another lender or lend on a ‘self-build’ property built/owned by a customer within the last 2 years. We also do not lend on incomplete self-builds.  

We can consider self-build properties in Northern Ireland where the current mortgage is with Ulster Bank. See our Northern Ireland Hub for more information. 

Shared ownership

We will lend on Shared Ownership/Co-ownership properties. Please see our Shared Ownership hub for more detail.

Solar panels

We’ll consider a property with solar panels subject to confirmation that installation has been completed by a Microgeneration Certification Scheme accredited contractor.

The below link provides a list of acceptable installation contractors:

MCS- Find a contractor

Where the solar panels are leased the solicitor will review the UK lenders Handbook.

Solar panels need to be installed by an MCS contractor where they are bought outright or leased.

Northern Ireland only - We don’t accept lease arrangements other than a Lease of Rights (not a standard lease).

Spray foam insulation

Can be considered subject to valuer’s comments on their own merits. Properties are considered on a case by case basis.

Property criteria: W

Warranties

Where a property has been built, converted or significantly renovated within the last 10 years, we will require a warranty to be in place. Please see our New Build Hub for acceptable warranties and further guidance.

Working farms, smallholdings and crofts

Unacceptable.

Title and tenure

Title

  • A first charge or standard security must be taken over the property. 

  • A charge over part of the registered title is not acceptable. 

  • Multiple Titles- Lending on the properties with multiple titles can be considered.

Tenure

Acceptable property tenure:

  • Absolute ownership (Scotland).

  • Freehold (flying) – please see Freehold flats / Flying freehold properties.

  • Leasehold.

  • Sub-Leasehold.

Unacceptable property tenure:

  • Commonhold.

Leasehold

Where the remaining leasehold term is less than 85 years, the value of the property will continue to reduce until the lease is extended. This may result in the valuation being less than the purchase price or estimated value. This could also result in the property being declined. 

In all instances, assessment will be made based on the valuer's comments. We require a minimum of 30 years to be remaining on the lease at the end of the mortgage term. However, some flexibility is allowed for properties in prime locations in central London, where the minimum lease left at the end of the mortgage should be 10 years. 

Ground Rent and Service Charge

Each case is assessed on its own individual merits. The valuer will consider the current value of the property and review period. Where escalating ground rents have short review periods and/or are high in relation to the property value this may have an impact on the valuation and mortgageability. 

Solicitors have a duty of care to ensure that any leasehold agreement is not onerous for the customer.

Restrictions on resale

NatWest will be advised by either the valuer or the Solicitor of any restrictions, often these will be detailed in a Section 106 agreement (Section 75 in Scotland). 

Please note, certain restrictions may still apply to Shared Ownership. We have specific guidance which can be found in the following document under England and Wales > Shared Ownership Requirements > Restrictions on the Disposal of the property in a Section 106 Agreement (or equivalent). This will be subject to full checks being conducted by the solicitor acting on the case.

The restrictions we can consider are: 

  • Retirement complexes where there is a restriction in the age of the owner or occupier, for example must be over age 50.  
  • Properties where occupation is restricted to only part of the year, for example 10/11 months provided the customer is using the property as a holiday/2nd home. 
  • Right to Buy - properties may have a restriction in the title whereby the owner has to offer the property back to the local council if selling This restriction lasts for 10 years As NatWest are supportive of the Right to Buy scheme. 
  • Agricultural Restrictions: 
  • Only considered acceptable on residential applications  
    • Maximum Loan to Value (LTV) is 50% 
    • There should be no evidence of agricultural or commercial activity.  
    • The applicant must be employed in an agricultural related job. However, their primary source of income must not be related to the mortgaged property. 

The restrictions we can’t consider:

  • An overage clause which may entitle the previous vendor to a share of the profit of a subsequent sale of part (or whole) of the property.  
  • Restrictions determining who can buy the property, for example people on a housing waiting list, people who have lived in the area for a certain period, people of certain professions.  
  • Restrictions on the selling price, e.g. the property must be sold for 20% under market value. 
     

This is not a definitive list of unacceptable restrictions. 

Environmental Issues

During the valuation process the valuer may identify environmental issues which must be detailed within the report. These may include: 

  • Asbestos. 

  • Contaminated land. 

  • Flooding and coastal erosion. 

  • Geological problems such as the presence of gases, coal and other forms of mining operations (past and present) etc. 

  • Invasion plants, including Japanese Knotweed.

The valuer must assess each case on its individual merits. They may request specialist reports to support them with their assessment.

Cladding

We rely upon the professional opinion of our valuers to confirm if a property shows the presence of potentially combustible cladding or if there are other fire safety concerns. Should they feel this is the case, where confirmation of remediation is not available through Government Remediation data or an EWS1/FRAEW is not already available for a building, the valuer may request an EWS1 form or FRAEW (Fire Risk Assessment of External Walls).

All valuations remain subject to valuer’s comments. 

England

For flats in buildings over 11m within England, we will check Government Remediation Data to confirm whether the property is:

•Eligible for funding under Government Remediation Schemes

•Eligible for funding under Developer Remediation Schemes or assessed as not requiring remediation

Where this is confirmed, no EWS1 form or Leaseholder Deed of Certificate will be required and the property will be acceptable subject to the valuer’s comments.

 

Where an EWS1 form / FRAEW is requested:

•Flats in buildings with an EWS1 rating A1, A2 or B1 or FRAEW confirming remediation isn't required, are acceptable as standard

•Flats in building over 11m within England with an EWS1 rating A3 or B2 or FRAEW confirming remediation is required are acceptable subject to a qualifying leaseholder deed of certificate.

When leaseholder deed of certificates are required on a purchase, these would need to be obtained from the Vendor.

Wales

Where an EWS1 form / FRAEW is requested:

•Flats in buildings with an EWS1 rating A1, A2 or B1 or FRAEW confirming remediation isn't required, are acceptable as standard

•Flats in building over 11m within Wales with an EWS1 rating A3 or B2 or FRAEW confirming remediation is required are acceptable and will be confirmed by the valuation report.

Scotland and Northern Ireland

Where an EWS1 form / FRAEW is requested:

•Flats in buildings with an EWS1 rating A1, A2 or B1 or FRAEW confirming remediation isn't required, are acceptable as standard.

•Flats in buildings with an EWS1 rating A3 or B2 or FRAEW confirming remediation is required, are not currently acceptable.

Additional information

Where an EWS1/FRAEW is not available, we may accept alternative forms of remediation evidence on a case by case basis. Alternative forms of remediation evidence include a letter of comfort from the developer or DLUHC (Department of Levelling Up Housing and Communities), in addition to a Leaseholder Deed of Certificate.

The FRAEW must:

•  Contain an executive Summary which clearly advises if remediation is required or if an EWS1 rating has been provided.

•  Be signed and have the signatories qualifications listed.

•  The building name and postcode on the FRAEW must match the application.

Please note: A fire risk assessment (FRA) is not a specialist report for external walls and will not be accepted.

Valuations

Valuations - Free Valuation Products

Remortgage- We provide a free valuation on properties valued up to £10 million. 

Purchase- We provide a free valuation on properties where the lower of the purchase price or value is less than or equal to £3 million.

  • The free valuation is applicable per property, rather than per application.

  • We will complete our own checks to make sure a property meets our lending criteria. This may not be a physical inspection of the property. We may use an Automated Valuation Model (AVM) or complete a desktop valuation. This check is for the banks use only. The customer will not receive a copy of the valuation report. 

  • We highly recommend that your customer considers carrying out their own checks to satisfy themselves of the property value and condition before completing on a purchase. 

You can find more information on mortgage valuations here. 

Valuation fees

A valuation will always be obtained using a panel surveyor instructed by us (NatWest). 

Where a valuation fee is payable, the fees are:

  • Up to £3 million                    £102

  • £3 million - £10 million       £1380

This fee is based on the lower of the purchase price or estimated value of the property.

The payment will be taken from the card details supplied on the application form.  If for any reason, payment can't be processed from these details the customer or broker will have to call 0345 600 0205 and make the payment.

For any properties valued over £10 million, valuations are available upon request via Legal and General Surveying Services.

Where a valuation fee is applicable, we will provide customers with a full valuation fee refund if their applications are declined on the grounds of affordability or credit scoring or where we can't lend the full amount requested due to affordability issues (unless they have requested to instruct a valuation at their own risk i.e a purchase application where the property value is over £3 million).

Valuation instruction

Valuations are instructed on Day 1. For applications where the purchase price is above £3 million or the property value is over £10 million (remortgage applications), our processing team will contact you prior to the valuation instruction and we adhere to the following process:

Where the valuation fee is applicable, the customer is given the choice between whether the valuation is instructed at that point at the customers own risk following receipt of application or wait until the case is fully agreed by an underwriter (Please note that if the customer does proceed and the valuation is instructed at customers own risk, they could lose the fee if the valuation is carried out but they withdraw.)

 

Please consult our Service Levels page for current processing timescales.

Valuation Challenges

Valuation challenges will now only be accepted where it meets one of the following criteria:

1)      Factual error on the valuation instruction e.g. number of bedrooms noted is incorrect. Please contact us to make us aware of any errors.

2)      The property is a New Build and there are relevant recent comparables on the same site, along with evidence of second hand properties and/or other new build properties on a separate development. For further guidance and to raise a valuation challenge, please see our L&G New Build Valuation Challenge Form.

Can the customer arrange a Home Buyers survey, Home Survey Level Two or Three?

We will only arrange a standard Valuation for Mortgages Purposes. 

Where the customer wants a more in-depth survey, they will need to arrange this themselves. 

Customers can use the RICS website to find a qualified surveyor. 

Alternatively, the customer can contact our partners Legal & General on 0345 200 2046

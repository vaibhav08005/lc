PAYE Contract Workers

We can help agency, fixed term contractors and umbrella contractors.  We’ll need to see:  

  • 1 year's evidence and a contract in place for minimum of a further 3 months. The 1 year's evidence can be as follows:
    • Two 6 month contracts.
    • Four 3 month contracts.
    • Latest 12 months consecutive payslips.

  • If your client is a High Income/Day Rate contractor please refer to this section below.

Zero Hours contractors/Piece workers

There must be a history of employment over the past 12 months and confirmation that a current contract is in place.

Income can be verified using the latest 3 months' payslips (total averaged and in line with the year to date figure) and latest P60. They must demonstrate consistent levels of income.

Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)

Whilst technically these applicants are self-employed for tax purposes, please key as Employed on the application. We will use the Gross income figure (minus expenses) shown over the last 12 months from the contract vouchers/statements. 

We’ll need to see:

  • Latest 12 months consecutive CIS slips and contract confirming ongoing employment.

Umbrella Company Contractor

We can help Umbrella Contractors. We know their income may be structured in different ways.

  • Employed: Please see PAYE Contract Workers

  • Day rate in excess of £75,000: Please see High Income Contractors / Day Rate Contractors 

It’s common to see salary/basic pay, holiday pay and Commission on your customer’s Payslip. For Umbrella Company Payslips, we will use the income after the Umbrella Company deduction has been made.

High Income Contractors / Day Rate Contractors

High Income Contractors can be contracting in different ways.

Expanding the sections below will help you identify how we can help your customer depending on how they contract.

Income eligibility for all High Income Contractors

Firstly, for Contractors earning over £75,000 we’ll calculate their income as their weekly contracted income multiplied by 46.

We accept variations of a contract’s pay rate e.g. hourly, daily or weekly etc. Please see below for common examples:

Contract history eligibility for all High Income Contractors

To apply the above income calculations, the customer must meet the following:

  • They will need to have contract(s) that cover a 12-month period. A minimum of 6 months’ contract(s) must have already been completed at the point of application.
  • They can’t have taken more than a 6 week break between contracts in the 12-month period.

Now look at the relevant section below, depending on how your customer is contracting.

Customers trading via a Limited Company

As well as the above Income and Contract History eligibility, customers trading via a Limited Company must meet one of the following:

  • Single applicant where they own 100% of the company.
  • Joint application where 1 applicant owns 100% of the company.
  • Joint application where the company is jointly owned.
  • Joint application where 1 applicant owns 100% of the company and the other applicant is employed by the company.

 

In addition, please see:

  • Significant business expenses as we deduct these from the income.
  • Evidence requirements for guidance on how to submit your application and what documents we will need you to provide.

Customers trading as a Sole Trader

If your customer meets the above Income and Contract History eligibility, they meet our high income policy.

In addition, please see:

  • Significant business expenses as we deduct these from the income.
  • Evidence requirements for guidance on how to submit your application and what documents we will need you to provide.

Customers on a fixed term contract or an umbrella company contract

If your customer meets the above Income and Contract History eligibility, they meet our high income policy.

In addition, please see:

  • Significant business expenses as we deduct these from the income.
  • Evidence requirements for guidance on how to submit your application and what documents we will need you to provide.

High Income Contractors / Day Rate Contractors continued

Significant business expenses we include for all High Income Contractors

Any significant business expenses that are not covered by the applicant’s contract must be fully understood, evidenced and accounted for in the affordability calculation, including:

  • Car/finance loans.
  • Extended travel and accommodation costs.
  • Training courses.
  • Professional indemnity insurance.
  • Partner/spouse/employee salaries (spouse’s income only needs to be factored in if their income is being included on the application under the ‘income’ section).

This is not an exhaustive list. 

Evidence requirements for all High Income Contractors

When submitting a High Income Contractor / Day Rate Contractor application, please key the applicant as Self-employed and input the same annualised day rate income figure in the ‘drawings/salary’ box for the last 2 years income which will be requested.

We’ll need:

  • Latest 3 months’ consecutive personal and business bank statements to help identify all significant business expenses.
  • A copy of the contract(s) to cover a 12-month period, where a minimum of 6 months’ contract(s) have already been completed at the point of application.

Where your customer is on or has recently switched to a fixed term contract or umbrella company contract, in addition to the above we’ll also need:

Sending us packaging: For new business and additional borrowing applications, the portal will ask you to upload Self-employed income verification. Instead, upload the specific documents listed above and include a memo to explain that the income you have declared is for a High Income Contractor.

If your customer doesn’t meet our High income Contractor eligibility criteria, we can still help

Where your customer can’t meet all the above eligibility and evidence requirements relevant to their contracting situation, please see the below instead:

  • For Self-employed customers: The relevant section on our self-employed hub.
  • For Employed customers (Fixed Term Contract or Umbrella Company Contract): Our PAYE contractor policy within this Hub.

FAQs

My customers contract is paid in a foreign currency. Can I still use the Day Rate contractor policy?

Yes, providing the currency is on our acceptable list, please see Foreign Currency. Where the customer is trading via a Limited company this must be registered in the UK or Republic of Ireland.  The customer also must be residing in the UK majority of the time, please see Working Abroad.

Our Buy to Let mortgages at a glance

We can help customers get onto the Buy to Let property ladder where they have never owned a property before, as well as helping customers who have a property portfolio.

Eligibility and criteria

Property requirements

  • The property must be in England, Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland.

  • A residential property which will not be used by the applicant or their family at any time. 

  • The Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) for the property must show a minimum rating of E. We will need to see a valid EPC on the EPC registers, see- The England, Wales & Northern Ireland Register or The Scotland Register.
  • Properties subject to a Selective Licensing Scheme can be considered subject to valuer’s comments on their own merits. Properties are considered on a case by case basis.

We can’t assist with:

  • Multiple occupancy (HMOs); multi-unit blocks; bedsits; multiple tenancies (for example, a house / flat with several bedrooms occupied by individuals who share common facilities including the bathroom and kitchen).

  • Lending to Business partnerships or limited companies.

  • Holiday lets.

Tenancy requirements

For remortgage cases, the property must be rented at time of application with rent evidenced in last 31 days for all remortgage applications (excluding Let to Buy cases).

 

Acceptable Tenancies:

Tenancy agreements must be held between private individuals and cannot be set up in a company name or with a company.

  • Assured Periodic Tenancy (APT) – England and Wales.
    • Where the rent is above £100,000 per year these can be considered subject to valuation and legal checks.

  • Private Residential Tenancy (PRT) – Scotland.

  • Private Tenancy Agreement – Northern Ireland.

 

We allow applications where the tenant is in receipt of Housing Benefit or Housing payment as part of their Universal Credit- Department of Social Security (DSS) tenants.

Applicant requirements

The applicants must be:

  • Resident in the UK at the time of application.
  • At least one applicant must either be a UK national, have permanent “Right to Reside”, indefinite leave to remain, Irish Citizenship, Settled or Pre-settled status. See our Foreign Nationals policy for more information.

Income

We have no minimum income* requirement for Buy to Let applications 

The customer must pass the applicable Buy to Let calculation and credit scoring process. Please see our Buy to Let affordability calculators.

*includes £0 income.

Tax banding

The tax band you select must take into account expected rental income from all properties the customer will own on completion of this application.

For joint applications where there are multiple tax bands, please select the highest tax band.

Where the tax banding appears incorrect, the underwriter may request further information.

We can’t give guidance on a customer's tax status. If the customer is unsure they should contact HMRC.

Let to Buy

We can accept Let to Buy applications and our standard BTL criteria applies.

Note - Changing from a residential to a BTL is not considered like-for-like.

Please key this as ‘Remo with AdBo or Let to Buy’ on our calculator.

Please see our guidance under Keying the application.

Consumer Buy to Let

Our approach to identifying a consumer Buy to Let is based on how the customer views their Buy to Let activity. Where the customer intends to benefit from house price growth or rental income we will class this as Investment purposes and not a consumer BTL. 

We don’t offer consumer BTL mortgages.

Sourcing Tip - If property will be used as an investment property, ensure that sourcing isn’t filtered by Consumer Buy to Let so that NatWest appears on your sourcing.

Regulated Buy to Let

A regulated BTL is where the property will be rented out to an immediate family member.

We don’t currently support regulated Buy to Let.

Additional borrowing

Where a customer requests additional borrowing on a buy to let property, applications can’t be considered in the following circumstances:

  • Gambling.

  • Any form of business purpose (i.e. start up for a new business but please note this does not include buying a buy to let in a limited company name).

  • We will not offer the customer any form of Unsecured Debt Consolidation.

If a customer is raising funds for an onward purchase, we need to know the intended property. The underwriter may request:

  • Where this will be mortgaged - a KFI or mortgage offer for the intended purchase. 

  • If this will be bought for cash- a copy of the memorandum of sale. 

  • An ARLA letter where a rental property is being purchased.

Keying the application

Rental income from either the subject or background properties should not be keyed as an income source on the Agreement in Principal or the Full Mortgage Application.

All background property information including from individual rental properties will be captured in the application.

For employed clients who are unable to verify their income in line with our packaging requirements income should be keyed as £0. 

Where the client has a Holiday Let in the background this will be included within our maximum portfolio criteria.

On our ‘Expenditure’ screen under ‘Other existing mortgages/properties’, please include all of the following as relevant on completion of this loan:

  • Main residence where it is unencumbered.

  • BTL/Consent to Let properties (mortgaged & unencumbered).

  • Additional residential properties (mortgaged & unencumbered).

  • New onward BTL or Residential properties being purchased (excluding this application).

 

UK only properties should be captured. Where there is a Non-UK property this should not be captured in this section, instead should the property be a holiday home / run at a loss e.g. the rental income received is less than the mortgage payment creating a shortfall, please key the shortfall amount on the application under Expenditure > Other financial commitments > Second property costs. 

Valuations

A physical valuation is required.

Rental income for the application will be based on the market rental as indicated on the valuation report or the rental received whichever is lower. 

Procuration fees

0.45% gross (both DA and AR firms). £250 minimum. Please see Procuration Fees.

Although we don’t require evidence of income and bank statements for small landlords, we’re still required to complete credit checks and keep accurate records.

Putting climate at the heart of our purpose

According to the Department for Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy, residential properties make up 16% of the UK total greenhouse gas emissions (UK Greenhouse gas emissions figures 2021). We want to help to lower this and reward customers who are trying to do the same.

Our ambition is for 50% of our mortgage base to have an EPC rating at or above C by 2030, to achieve alignment with the 2015 Paris Agreement.

Visit our Climate Change Hub

At a glance

The offer is only available on selected mortgages marked with ‘Green Mortgage - Cashback’ and can be changed or withdrawn at any point.

  • Reduced rates on a 2-year or 5-year fixed-rate mortgage for energy efficient homes.
  • Where applicable, cashback will be paid to their solicitor on the day they drawdown their mortgage.
  • Maximum LTVs: 
    • Residential products up to 85%. 
    • Buy-to-let products up to 75%.
  • Not applicable for Shared Equity or Help to Buy applications.
  • Product fees may apply.

Early repayment charges and all standard NatWest lending rules and policies, including our Buy -to-Let policy, applies.

Eligibility criteria

  • Property Must be in the UK.
  • Available on Purchases (including porting) and Remortgages.
  • There must be a valid Energy Performance Certificated (EPC) listed on the registers detailed below with a rating of A or B.
  • We will also consider customers buying a new build property.

Properties where there is currently only a proposed or anticipated EPC rating of A or B are not eligible.

No other EPC data registers or documentation, for example a Predicted Energy Assessment (PEA), will be accepted.

Before submitting

  • It is the broker's responsibility to ensure the appropriately rated A or B certificate is in place
  • Energy Performance Certificate must be valid within 10 years of production
  • No additional documentation to our standard application packaging requirements needs to be submitted

Registers and New Build eligibility

For Energy Performance Certificates please contact:

  • The England, Wales & Northern Ireland Register
  • The Scotland Register

You can also get a Green Mortgage if your customer is buying a new build property.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you define a New Build for automatic Green eligibility?

Any New build property built within the last two years will be eligible for green mortgages.

If a property was built more than two years ago, but has been first occupied, significantly modernised or refurbished within the last two years as EPC rating of A or B will need to be evidenced to be eligible.

Who is responsible for ensuring my customers Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) is listed on the acceptable Government registers and rated A or B prior to application submission?

It is the brokers responsibility, by checking the relevant Government registers, prior to submission.

Acceptable EPC registers?

Click here for acceptable EPC Registers

I have checked the appropriate acceptable EPC register and the property does not have an EPC rating confirmed?

If the property does not have an EPC rating confirmed on the correct register then unfortunately we are unable to offer the customer a Green Mortgage 

**IMPORTANT- Please check you have accessed the correct country data register **

My customer disputes the EPC rating or has an EPC that differs from the appropriate acceptable register?

If your customer disputes the EPC please explain that we only use the Government register as our source of information and if the details they have differ from the Government website please inform them that they will need to ask the assessor who carried out their EPC to update the Government website – the seller or their estate agent maybe able to help with this.

Are there any additional packaging requirements for a Green Mortgage and Remortgage?

No, however please ensure you have checked the appropriate acceptable register to confirm that the EPC is A or B rated. In addition you will be required to tick a box on the mortgage application form confirming applying for a Green Mortgage

Can a green mortgage be ported?

Customers can port their Green Mortgage to any property without paying an Early Repayment Charge however any additional borrowing required to purchase the new property must meet A/B EPC eligibility to be offered another Green Mortgage product.

Please contact our Business Development Team to arrange this.

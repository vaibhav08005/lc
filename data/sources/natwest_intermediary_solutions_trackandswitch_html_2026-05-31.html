Track and Switch

Track and switch information

ONLY FOR USE BY MORTGAGE INTERMEDIARIES

On this page
What is our Track and Switch facility?
Frequently asked questions

What is our Track and Switch facility?

Our Track and Switch facility allows customers who are uncertain about fixing their rate right now to choose a Tracker mortgage and fix their mortgage rate later.  

It’s simple.

If it’s right for your customer, they can take any Tracker mortgage now. NatWest Tracker mortgages are variable rate products which track the National Westminster Bank Plc base rate.  You’ll get paid your standard procurement fee for any new or existing business tracker cases.

Then, if they wish to switch to a Fixed Rate mortgage, you can use our Product Transfer system to switch them easily onto the Fixed Rate they’d like.  They can do this once their tracker mortgage has drawn down, they won’t be re-credit scored and you'll receive a standard Product Transfer procuration fee.  

Frequently asked questions

What is a Tracker mortgage?

A Tracker mortgage is a product with a variable rate that tracks the National Westminster Bank Plc base rate. This means that the customers mortgage rate and amount they pay for their mortgage on a monthly basis can change. An increase could see the rate your customer pays increase too.  Likewise, if the rate goes down, the rate your customer pays should follow

What is the National Westminster Bank Plc base rate?

The National Westminster Bank Plc base rate is an independent variable rate.   

What is the difference between the Bank of England Base Rate and the National Westminster Bank Plc base rate?

The Bank of England is the central bank of the United Kingdom and their job is to ensure a stable and strong economy. They set their own base rate which in turn influences the rate lenders are able to offer for products like mortgages. Changes in the Bank of England Base Rate are one of the main reasons we change our National Westminster Bank Plc Base Rate, but it is not the only reason. We have full control over our own base rate and this could go up or down regardless of what happens to the Bank of England base rate

What is Track and Switch?

Track and Switch is a facility that allows a customer to take a Tracker mortgage now and switch onto a Fixed Rate mortgage later. The customer will not be re-credit checked or scored. 

The customer can switch onto a Natwest fixed rate after the tracker mortgage has drawn down. To see what rates are available to your customer, you can log in using their details and we’ll show you what deals you can choose from today. All Tracker products may be switched to a Natwest fixed rate product without incurring the Early Repayment Charge, subject to meeting the new product criteria and availability at the time of application. The product switch is subject to any fee(s) applicable to the new product at that time. The new fixed rate product may also have an Early Repayment Charge.

Please note: If your customer is on a Lifetime Tracker rate, they will need to contact us directly to apply for a product transfer.

How does it work?

Providing the customer has drawn down on their tracker mortgage, when the customer wants to switch on to a Fixed Rate product, this works like a Product Transfer.  Terms apply.  There is no additional credit scoring or underwriting.  The customer can choose any product providing they meet the criteria for the product i.e. LTV, remaining term etc.  

Who is it for?

It is for customers who want to take advantage of a Tracker mortgage now but may want to fix their mortgage rate later.   It is for those customers who are comfortable with a variable rate and payment which can change monthly.

What are the latest Tracker and Fixed rates?

To see what product transfer rates are available to your client, you can log in using their details and we’ll show you what deals they can choose from today.

How long does my customer have to be on a Tracker mortgage before they can fix their rate?

The customer needs to be drawn down on the tracker product before they can switch into a Fixed Rate mortgage.

Frequently asked questions

How do I do this?

Once your customer has drawn down onto their Tracker mortgage you will be able to switch them via our Product Transfer system. 

How do I get paid for a new business customer?

If the customer is a new customer to NatWest when you recommend a Tracker mortgage you will be paid your standard procuration fee.  If your customer chooses to switch to a Fixed Rate mortgage you’ll be paid for doing a product transfer.  Please check your own or your network and clubs’ terms for the latest procuration fees.  

How will this work for a Product Transfer?

If the customer is doing a Product Transfer with NatWest and you recommend a Tracker mortgage, you will be paid your Product Transfer procuration fee.  If your customer then chooses to switch to a Fixed Rate mortgage you’ll be paid a Product Transfer procuration fee. Please check your own or your network and clubs’ terms for the latest procuration fees.

Will switching incur an Early Repayment Charge?

All Tracker products may be switched to any NatWest fixed rate product, subject to meeting the new product criteria and availability at the time of application and without incurring the Early Repayment Charge.

Can customers on a track and switch deal, switch to a fixed rate at the same time they port their mortgage?

A customer can switch to a fixed deal without incurring an Early Repayment Charge while porting.

  • The ‘Are you porting an existing NatWest product’ question should be answered as "No" as a new product will be selected.
  • Our New Business fixed rate products should be used.

Note: Where another sub-account is already on a fixed rate and this is to be ported, then key as a porting case, as normal.

Is Additional Borrowing allowed when switching to a fixed rate?

Yes, they can apply as early as after the 1st months payment on their new mortgage.

What happens in a product transfer if the customer is in the rolloff window?

If the customer’s tracker deal is coming to an end and they have entered the roll off window, you will be given the option to start the deal straight away or when the customers current deal ends. There will be no ERC charged for switching immediately. This includes tracker to tracker products if available. (Tracker to tracker is only available within the roll off window)

Frequently asked questions

My client is not yet within their product transfer window but wants additional borrowing. They’d like both products to have aligned end dates — what are the options?

You can consider our Track & Switch facility. This allows the additional borrowing to be placed on a tracker product initially. Once the client enters their product transfer window, both the existing mortgage and the additional borrowing can be switched to a new fixed rate product with aligned end dates. Please ensure you recommend the most suitable option based on your client's individual needs and circumstances.

If I have secured a new rate to start when the current tracker deal ends, can I bring the start date of that deal forward?

No. It you want to start a new deal immediately then you need to cancel the application in full and reapply. 

Can I delay the start date of the new deal if I am doing a product transfer using the track and switch facility?

No. If you are using the track and switch facility the new deal will start immediately.
